Chase weather data January 2013 to March 2025
        Last edit 17 June 2025 Dave Spittlehouse, Victoria, BC 

This document is a description of the Excel spreadsheets and pdf file in the directory 

Daily: Daily data (January 1 to Dec 31) - average, maximum, minimum, total, spot reading at midnight. 
      Spreadsheet name includes the year, i.e., Chase2013DY.xlsx

Hourly: Hourly data (January 1 to Dec 31) - average, total and maximum. 
      Evaluation of solar radiation for decrease in sensitivity and shading.
      Spreadsheet name includes the year, i.e., Chase2013H.xlsx

Sumry: ChaseDailySumry.xlxs is a spreadsheet of the summary columns of the measured and gap filled daily data for all years. 
      
Report: Status of the Chase weather station - Chase Weather Station Evaluation.pdf.

Daily data spreadsheets
There are two worksheets in each spreadsheet (Data, Meta) and graph sheets by variable name 
Meta data worksheet: Explains measurement procedures (equipment and variables), data issues and gap filling.
Data worksheet: Measured and gap filled data. Comparison graphs and regressions for gap filling.
      Summary of the data rounded to one or two decimals, and includes gap filling. In columns from BA to DZ depending on the year. 
      Does not include logger temperature or wind direction.

Hourly data spreadsheets
There are two worksheets in each spreadsheet (Data, Meta). 
Meta data worksheet: Explains measurement procedures (equipment and variables), data issues and gap filling.
Data worksheet: Measured and gap filled data. Algorithm and graphs for evaluation of solar radiation for decrease in sensitivity and shading.

Gap-filling of variables: Negligible amount. The missing 1.5 m air temperature is not filled. 
	Snow depth has some minor gap-filling in 2016-2018 with 0.25 m air temperature and soil temperature used to indicate snow pack.

Winter precipitation is determined from snow depth measurement at midnight at the site and a estimate of
    rain on snow.  
    Increase in snow depth over 24 hours converted to mm water assuming snow settled to density of 0.13.
    Rain on snow is based on the tipping bucket data and air temperature. Discount data for melted snow based on snowfall estimate from depth gauge.
